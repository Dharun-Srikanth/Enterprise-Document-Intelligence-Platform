Financial Summary Report — Q3 2025

Prepared by: Sarah Chen, VP Finance

Distribution: Executive Leadership Team

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Nexus Automotive Group achieved total revenue of $142.8M in Q3 2025, representing a 7.2% year-over-year increase. Operating expenses were controlled at $118.4M, yielding an operating margin of 17.1%. The cost optimization initiative led by the procurement team delivered $8.3M in savings against a target of $7.5M.

COST OPTIMIZATION PROGRAM — PROCUREMENT TOWER

The procurement tower cost-saving initiative, managed by David Park (Director of Procurement), identified 23 actionable ideas across four categories:

1. Material Substitution: $3.2M in potential savings by switching from cold-rolled steel to HSLA steel for seat frame structures. Carbon footprint reduction estimated at 22%.

2. Supplier Consolidation: $2.1M savings through consolidating seat track assembly suppliers from 5 to 2 preferred vendors (TierOne Manufacturing and SteelCraft Industries).

3. Design Optimization: $1.8M savings from redesigning the slide rail mechanism to reduce material waste by 18%. Engineering team (led by Raj Patel) completed feasibility study.

4. Process Improvement: $1.2M savings from lean manufacturing implementation at the Greenville plant. Timeline: Q1 2026 completion.

CAPITAL EXPENDITURE

Total CapEx for Q3: $12.6M
- New stamping press (Greenville plant): $4.8M
- Automation upgrade (Detroit assembly line): $3.2M
- Quality inspection systems: $2.1M
- IT infrastructure: $2.5M

SUSTAINABILITY METRICS

Carbon emissions for Q3: 18,400 tonnes CO2e (down 5.3% from Q3 2024)
Key driver: Material substitution program reducing steel processing emissions.
Target: 15% reduction by end of 2026, aligned with SBTi commitment.

OUTLOOK

Q4 2025 revenue projected at $148.5M based on confirmed orders. The board approved an additional $5M investment in sustainability R&D, specifically targeting alternative materials with lower embodied carbon for seat frame and recliner components.
