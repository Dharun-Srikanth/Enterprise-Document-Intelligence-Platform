PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Cost Optimization & Sustainability Integration Program (COSIP) is a cross-functional initiative to achieve $15M in annual cost savings while reducing the carbon footprint of manufactured components by 20% over 24 months. The program spans procurement, engineering, manufacturing, and sustainability functions.

Budget: $4.2M allocated (FY2025-2026)
Timeline: January 2025 — December 2026
Current Phase: Phase 2 — Material Substitution & Supplier Optimization

PHASE 2 DETAILED STATUS

Workstream 2A — Seat Components Material Substitution
Lead: Raj Patel (Engineering) + David Park (Procurement)

Progress:
- Seat track assembly: HSLA steel option validated. Cost delta: +$2.00/unit. Carbon reduction: -14%. Production approval pending weld validation (Risk R-042).
- Seat frame structure: Tubular steel evaluation complete. Cost savings: $1.50/unit vs cold-rolled steel. Carbon reduction: -11%. Approved for Phase 3 pilot.
- Recliner mechanism: Testing in progress. Preliminary results show HSLA steel viable with 10% weight reduction. Expected completion: Sep 15, 2025.

Workstream 2B — Supplier Optimization
Lead: David Park

Progress:
- Seat track suppliers consolidated from 5 to 2 (TierOne Manufacturing, SteelCraft Industries).
- TierOne audit PASSED (Aug 2025). SteelCraft audit scheduled for Sep 10, 2025.
- Estimated procurement savings: $2.1M annually.
- Anika Johansson confirmed both suppliers meet Scope 3 emissions reporting requirements.

Workstream 2C — Sustainability Analytics Platform
Lead: Anika Johansson

Progress:
- Carbon footprint benchmark database created for all 5 component families and 5 material types.
- Life cycle assessment (LCA) methodology aligned with ISO 14044.
- Dashboard prototype under development — integrates cost and emissions data for decision support.
- Data handling policy signed by all stakeholders. Sanitization approach: PII removal from scanned documents before ingestion into analytics platform.
